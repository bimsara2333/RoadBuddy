About RoadBuddy

RoadBuddy is a comprehensive Machine Learning-based Driver Assistant System (DAS) designed to address key challenges in Sri Lanka's driving landscape. Our mission is to significantly reduce traffic accidents by tackling issues like reckless driving, insufficient road sign detection, and poor lane discipline.

Objectives

Road safety is a pervasive concern in Sri Lanka, where the rate of traffic accidents has led to significant loss of life and property. Despite various governmental initiatives aimed at improving road safety, the need for innovative solutions remains evident.

RoadBuddy is a comprehensive Machine Learning-based Driver Assistant System (DAS) designed to mitigate key challenges in the driving landscape of Sri Lanka, including reckless driving, insufficient road sign detection, and poor lane discipline.

The Problem

According to the World Health Organization, road traffic accidents are the leading cause of injury death among youth aged 5–29 years, and the seventh leading cause of death for all ages across the globe. Sri Lanka, like most low- and middle-income countries, is greatly impacted by road safety due to irresponsible driving, poor road conditions, and poor enforcement of traffic regulations.

Our Solution

RoadBuddy is a comprehensive solution that includes several key features designed to enhance road safety:

  • Virtual instructor provides real-time feedback and guidance to drivers
  • Biometric verification for driver identification and personalized assistance
  • Parking assistance to detect available spots and assist with correct alignment
  • Enhanced road sign detection to keep drivers informed of traffic conditions
  • Blind spot detection to alert drivers about vehicles or obstacles
  • Driver monitoring system to detect drowsiness, distraction, or fatigue

The significance of this research lies in its potential to substantially reduce traffic accidents in Sri Lanka by addressing the root causes of unsafe driving. By integrating advanced technologies such as machine learning, RoadBuddy offers a proactive approach to road safety.

Literature Review


A review of global and local research highlighting the importance of ADAS and RoadBuddy’s innovative approach to road safety.

The integration of intelligent technologies in modern vehicles has become pivotal for enhancing driver safety, security, and convenience. Extensive research has been carried out across several domains of advanced driver assistance systems (ADAS), including driver identification and monitoring, blind spot detection, parking assistance, road sign recognition, and multimodal alert systems. These components, while traditionally explored in isolation, are increasingly being combined into unified systems for holistic support and accident prevention.

Driver Identification and Monitoring systems play a crucial role in ensuring that only authorized individuals operate a vehicle while also maintaining driver attention and alertness throughout the journey. Studies have demonstrated the effectiveness of monitoring driver behavior to detect distraction and drowsiness using machine learning. However, these systems often fail to address driver identity verification. In contrast, biometric identification methods such as facial recognition and voice authentication contribute to secure access but are limited in providing continuous verification throughout a trip.

Recent advances leverage deep learning to enhance driver monitoring. These include affect recognition for emotional state analysis, CNN-based facial recognition with real-time monitoring, and complementary technologies such as infrared and thermal imaging to ensure performance under challenging conditions. Multimodal biometric integration further strengthens these systems by combining facial, voice, and fingerprint data for robust verification.

In addition, predictive behavior analysis using physiological data—such as heart rate and skin conductance—and in-vehicle sensor integration provide real-time monitoring of driver readiness and focus. These holistic approaches enhance the reliability and safety of modern driving experiences.

Blind Spot Detection has evolved rapidly, especially in traffic-dense regions. Deep learning and computer vision have enabled real-time object detection in hidden zones, while radar technologies offer robustness in adverse weather. Multi-sensor fusion techniques and regional adaptations improve detection accuracy and usability across various conditions.

Parking Assistance systems have matured from basic ultrasonic sensors to intelligent, AI-driven solutions. Innovations include 3D modeling, hybrid sensor integration, and autonomous parking functionalities tailored to different vehicle types and environments.

Road Sign Recognition systems have seen major progress due to CNNs and GANs, addressing real-world variability and complex traffic scenarios. Climate condition monitoring using sensor fusion further strengthens the reliability of ADAS in low-visibility conditions.

Multimodal alert systems combining visual and auditory cues improve driver reaction times, reducing cognitive load. Unified ADAS platforms are aligned with the development of Intelligent Transportation Systems (ITS), enabling predictive analytics and real-time adaptability.

Despite these advancements, challenges remain in ensuring reliability, cybersecurity, and ethical compliance. However, the convergence of machine learning, sensor technologies, and user-centered design continues to shape the future of safer, smarter vehicles.

Results & Impact

RoadBuddy has demonstrated significant performance improvements in driver safety and behavior through rigorous testing.

System Performance

Driver Identification & Monitoring 89%
Blind Spot Detection 92%
Traffic Sign Recognition 94%
Parking Assistance 90%

The overall system demonstrated excellent performance across various lighting conditions and traffic loads, showing its versatility and robustness.

User Feedback

Increased driving confidence 84%

Percentage of users who reported increased confidence while driving, particularly in complex or dangerous situations.

Improved driving discipline 78%

Percentage of users who indicated that the system's real-time alerts and guidance helped them become more conscious and disciplined drivers.

Areas for Improvement

  • • GPS signal reception issues in busy urban areas
  • • Audio-visual feedback complexity for some users
  • • Performance in extreme weather conditions
